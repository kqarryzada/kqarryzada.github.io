I graduated from The University of Texas at Austin in 2017 with an undergraduate degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering, with focuses on Computer Architecture and Software Engineering. During my time there, I worked at The Texas Petawatt Laser Lab (also known as CHEDS), where I developed several programs used to obtain measurements and statuses of pressure gauges and gate valves.

Currently, I'm working at Ping Identity as a sustaining engineer on the PingDirectory team. I assist on support cases involving a wide range of topics, including replication, JVM garbage collection, and tuning of configuration elements such as the heap to improve performance. My favorite cases to work on typically involve lower-level concepts, such as using memory analyzing tools to determine the cause of memory leaks in customer deployments.

When I'm not coding, I enjoy spending my time bouldering, reading, and playing video games. My favorite game of all time is Team Fortress 2.
